# Key Findings — AeroFit Customer Segmentation

## 1. Product Market Share
| Product | Customers | % of Total |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| KP281 (Entry $1,500) | 80 | 44.4% |
| KP481 (Mid $1,750) | 60 | 33.3% |
| KP781 (Premium $2,500) | 40 | 22.2% |

The entry-level KP281 captures nearly half the market. The premium KP781 has the smallest buyer base but the most distinctive customer profile.

---

## 2. Average Customer Profile by Product
| Product | Avg Age | Avg Income | Avg Fitness | Avg Usage/week | Avg Miles/week |
|---------|---------|------------|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| KP281 | 28.6 | $46,418 | 3.0 / 5 | 3.1x | 82.8 |
| KP481 | 28.9 | $48,974 | 2.9 / 5 | 3.1x | 87.9 |
| KP781 | 29.1 | $75,442 | 4.6 / 5 | 4.8x | 166.9 |

**Key insight:** KP281 and KP481 buyers look almost identical in age, income, and fitness. The KP781 buyer is a completely different profile — 63% higher income, significantly higher fitness (4.6 vs 3.0), and runs twice the miles per week.

---

## 3. Income Distribution
- KP281 and KP481 buyers are overwhelmingly in the **Middle income tier ($40k–$75k)** — 71% and 85% respectively.
- KP781 buyers split between **Middle** (19 buyers, avg $58k) and **High income** (21 buyers, avg $91k). No KP781 buyer has low income.
- **No low-income buyers purchased the KP781** — price is a clear barrier.

---

## 4. Fitness Level (the strongest predictor of product choice)
| Product | High Fitness (4-5) | Moderate (3) | Low (1-2) |
|---------|--------------------|--------------|-----------|
| KP281 | 13.8% | 67.5% | 18.8% |
| KP481 | 13.3% | 65.0% | 21.7% |
| KP781 | **90.0%** | 10.0% | 0.0% |

**This is the single strongest signal in the data.** 90% of KP781 buyers rate themselves as high fitness. KP281 and KP481 are nearly identical at ~14% high fitness buyers. Fitness level separates premium buyers from everyone else far more clearly than income alone.

---

## 5. Gender Patterns
- KP281 and KP481 are split roughly **50/50 male-female**.
- KP781 is **82.5% male** — a significant skew. Only 7 of 40 premium buyers are female.
- This suggests either a targeting gap or a genuine preference difference worth investigating.

---

## 6. Marital Status
- Marital status is **consistent across all products** — roughly 60% partnered, 40% single.
- This is not a useful differentiator for product targeting.

---

## 7. Upsell Opportunity
**3 KP281 buyers** have income >$50k and fitness >= 4 — they match the profile of a premium buyer but chose the entry-level product. These are candidates for an upgrade offer or targeted outreach.
